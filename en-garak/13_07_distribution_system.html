    Wholesale Market Distribution System Guide
    1. Auction-Based Market
    2. Shipment Guide
    1. Step 01
      Request for sale of agricultural products to a wholesale market corporation
    2. Step 02
      with the invoice (the document with details of the products).
    3. Step 03
      the wholesale market corporation sells the requested products by auction (pricing)
    4. Step 04
      The price is made known to the shipper by fax or telephone,
    5. Step 05
      and the calculated amount of money will be transferred to the shipper’s account.
    • It does not matter if the shipper of the agricultural product shipment is an individual or group (National Agricultural Cooperative Federation, cooperative units, farming association, etc.). The shipper can just send the products and their invoice (statement of items) together.
    • The standard invoice for agriculture products is kept in each wholesale market corporation. Refer to the following picture for the shipment process.
    • A shipper can propose in writing the lowest price that he/she is to accept for trading at Garak Market, and a wholesale market corporation shall not sell such products at a price lower than such price. (Relevant law: Article 33, Paragraph 1 of the ACT ON DISTRIBUTION AND PRICE STABILIZATION OF AGRICULTURAL AND FISHERY PRODUCTS and Article 29 of the ENFORCEMENT REGULATIONS OF THE ACT ON DISTRIBUTION AND PRICE STABILIZATION OF AGRICULTURAL AND FISHERY PRODUCTS)

    Functions by Distribution Entity
    구분, 주요역할 등이 포함된 표
    Type Key Roles
    Wholesale Market Corporation
    • Receive agricultural products from sellers/shippers and distribute them, or buy those products for resale (gathering products, auction, settlement for accounts)
    Market Wholesaler
    • The corporation (incorporated) buys agricultural products from producers and resells them, or producers consign agricultural products to the corporation for distribution
    Middlemen
    • The wholesale market corporation participates in an auction for listed agricultural products, buys them, and sells them. Or it acquires a trade license from a purchase broker and a person who sets up an agricultural products wholesale market for unlisted transaction (intermediary
    Trading Participants
    • Purchase agricultural and marine products by participating in an auction as an end user, like a processor, retailer, exporter, etc.
    Middlemen
    • Purchase agricultural and marine products from intermediary wholesalers in the market and sell them to retailers and consumers
